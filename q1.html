Finding the Path
When Your Child Needs More Than You Know How to Give

This questionnaire is the foundation of a resource for parents navigating behavioral and psychological challenges with their children. Your honest answers — no matter how incomplete or uncertain — are exactly what we need.

Section 01
The Beginning
Early childhood, birth, developmental milestones, and the first signs that something might be different. Don't worry about being medically precise — write what you remember and how it felt.
NICU stays, birth complications, early health concerns — include whatever feels relevant, even if you're not sure it matters.
This might be a specific moment, or just a gradual feeling that something was off. Both are valid.
Divorce, moves, job changes, other family stress. No judgment — life happens.
Section 02
The Diagnosis
How you got here — the process of finding out what your child was dealing with, and what happened right after.
Did they take you seriously, refer you somewhere, suggest waiting it out, or offer their own explanation? How long did you rely on their guidance before seeking a specialist?
A school counselor, a pediatrician, a therapist, a psychiatrist, a hospital? Was it through the school system or privately?
Be honest — even if the plan seemed reasonable at the time but didn't pan out.
Section 03
The Help You Tried
The therapists, counselors, programs, medications, and approaches you used — what worked, what didn't, and what you wish you'd known.
Were there signs at the time that something wasn't working, or did it take years to realize?
If you haven't yet, skip this or write about why — that's valuable information too.
Section 04
Where You Are Now
The current situation — what's working, what you're still figuring out, and how you're holding up.
This matters. Burned-out parents can't advocate well. Be honest.
Section 05
What You Wish Someone Had Told You
The most important section. This is the heart of the resource we're building.
