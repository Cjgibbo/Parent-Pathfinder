Parent Pathfinder

My Grounding Document
For the days you've lost track of who you are in all of this

This is not advice. It's not a form. It's yours. Answer honestly — nobody sees this but you. Print it when you're done and keep it somewhere real.

Nothing you write here is sent anywhere. This page has no submit button. Your answers stay on your screen until you print or close the tab.
01
Who were you as a parent before all of this got this hard?

What did you believe parenting was going to look like? What kind of parent did you set out to be?

02
What do you know to be true about your child — underneath the behavior?

Not what they do on the hard days. Who are they? What have you seen in them that nobody else gets to see?

03
What are you most afraid of for their future?

Name it. The fear you carry quietly. The thing you don't say out loud because it feels too big.

04
What are you trying to build for them — even when it feels impossible?

The version of their future you're still fighting for. Even on days when you can barely see it.

05
What has this cost you — honestly?

Sleep. Relationships. Your sense of self. Things you've given up. Things you've lost. You're allowed to name it.

06
What have you done right — even when it didn't feel like it?

The moments you showed up anyway. The times you came back after a hard one. Evidence you can point to that you are not the story you tell yourself on the worst days.

The one thing
07
The one thing you want to remember on the worst days.

Not a quote. Not advice. Yours. In your words. The thing that is true even when nothing else feels true.
